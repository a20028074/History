Turning Point: The Cultural Revolution

Preface

Mao's apprehension of the USSR's new leadership and policies and other factors led to high tension between China and the USSR. By the early 1960s, the tension was so high that Mao devised a plan that would:

  • Phase out any government officials that didn't share Mao's beliefs about how China's government.
  • Influence the youth to make them more revolutionary
  • Make healthcare, education, etc. less "elitist"

His plan for doing all of this focused primarily on the nation's children through a program called the "Red Guards."

The Red Guards

Beginning

Mao initiated the Cultural Revolution in May 1966, closing down the schools and influencing the Chinese people (mostly the children) through media to rebel against their leadership. Under the belief that "political power grows out of the barrel of a gun," an expression constructed and pushed by Mao, many elders and educated people/scholars/intellectuals were physically and mentally abused or killed. The destruction and terror among the general population harmed the economy and caused industrial production to go down 12% from 1966 to 1968.
